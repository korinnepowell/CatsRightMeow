Bengal Cat Breed

The Bengal cat breed is a domesticated cat created from cross-breeding the Asian leopard cat with the spotted Egyptian Mau. So this breed is a direct descendant of wildcats and domesticated cats. These cats have a wild appearance, with coats of marbling, rosettes, spots, and arrowhead markings across shimmery golden fur. Bengal cats have a slightly longer lifespan than the average domesticated cat, between 10-16 years. These cats are also slightly larger than the average house cat since selective breeding has been used in an attempt to get a Bengal cat closer in size to the original Asian leopard cat. Bengal cats are also considered a hypoallergenic breed and less likely to cause an allergic reaction in people since they produce lower levels of allergens.

Bengal Personality & Temperment

The Bengal cat breed is a highly energetic breed of cat. They are intelligent, energetic, and playful cats and need an owner that can match their spirit. They have been known to retrieve items and enjoy playing in the water. These cats need a lot of regular play sessions, designated climbing areas, and interactive toys. This will also help promote a healthy lifestyle and prevent obesity. This cat breed is also very affectionate with its owners and makes excellent family pets. In between play sessions, they love cuddles.

Health Concerns

A major health concern with the Bengal breed is Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy or (HCM). This disease makes the heart muscle abnormally thick, making it hard for the cat's heart to pump blood. These cats are also affected by a severe genetic disease called Bengal progressive retinal atrophy or PRA. Any breeder breeds these cats should carry this test out on all of their cats since it is inexpensive, noninvasive, and easy to perform. They should also offer the buyer proof of a negative result.

Bengal Legal Information

In 1983, the International cat association officially accepted the Bengal cat breed. However, there are some legal restrictions to owning a Bengal cat in the United States since it is a hybrid of a domestic and wildcat. In Hawaii and New York owning a Bengal cat is prohibited. In other areas like Colorado, Connecticut, Georgia, Massachusetts, Delaware, and Indiana, there are limits on Bengal ownership. Most require the cat to be at least four generations removed from its wild relative, or permits are required to own this type of cat, so it is good to know state laws before purchasing a Bengal cat.
