Himalayan Persian Cat Breed

The Himalayan Persian cat breed, or Himmie for short, is a long-haired cat breed similar in type to the Persian with signature blue eyes and point coloration. The Himalayan Persian is a relatively new breed developed in the United States and is a mix of Siamese and Persian cats. The average Himalayan cat has a height of 10-12 inches or 25-30cm and a length of 17-19inches or 43-48cm. An adult Himmie will usually weigh between 7-12 lbs, and their lifespan ranges between 9-15years. Himalayans are known for their long white-cream silky coats with darker color points that occur on the legs, face, and paws. Sometimes these color points, like seal points, can be significant, making the fur color look darker all over the body. Often kittens are born with little to no color and will develop full colorations over time.

Himalayan Persian Personality & Temperment

Himalayan Persian cats make excellent companions. These cats are intelligent, friendly, gentle, and affectionate. They are good with kids and other pets or are okay with being alone. This feline breed is tuned in to their owners and known for meeting them at the door. These cats are very relaxed and more sedate than most breeds; however, that does not mean they do not play, just that they are less likely to climb curtains and get into trouble like other breeds. Overall these cats adore attention and affection from their owners and have sweet temperate personalities.
