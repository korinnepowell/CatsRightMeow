Abyssinian Cat Breed

The Abyssinian cat, or Aby cat for short, is a domestic short-haired breed with a ticked tabby coat. This means that each hair has bands of different colors and is a trademark of the breed. Ticked hair is known as the agouti effect and is a genetic variant of the tabby pattern making the hair a uniform color all over the body. However, the ridge of the spine and tip of the tail, the back of the hind legs, the paws, and the pads are always noticeably darker. The Abyssinian has a lifespan of 9-15 years and can grow to a height of 7.9- 9.8 inches or 20-25 cm. This cat breed has a medium-sized, lean body, wedge-shaped head, and large, almond eyes. There will also be the signature M shape on the forehead of an Abyssinian.

Abyssinian Personality & Temperment

The Abyssinian cat makes for an excellent family pet. They are very intelligent, active, playful, and social; these cats love jumping, climbing, and interacting with their owners and other pets. These cats have almost dog-like characteristics with their desire for affection and interaction with their owner. This excellent quality can also be negative when purchasing an Abyssinian since they need a companian like another cat and should not be left alone for too long. The Aby cat is usually a quiet breed; however, rather than the classic meow, they have soft chirrup-like vocalizations.

Health Concerns

This breed is prone to gingivitis which can lead to more severe periodontitis. They also have been known for kidney disorders like Familial renal amyloidosis and AA amyloidosis due to a mutation in the AA protein gene. They can also have issues with hereditary blindness, which is why choosing a reputable breeder is critical.

Fun Fact

Regarding the domestication of cats, it has been found that the Abyssinian is one of the oldest types of domestic animal in existence, with mummified cats found in Egyptian tombs and were considered to be related to the gods by ancient Egyptians.
